Mechanical Design Objectives </h3> <p>The cone functions as a primary transduction surface converting electrical signal into acoustic pressure.</p> <ul> <li>Controlled cone breakup behavior at high SPL</li> <li>Low stored-energy resonance in midrange frequencies</li> <li>Stable transient response under dynamic load</li> </ul> </section> <section style="margin-bottom: 20px;"> <h3 style="background:#000;color:#fff;padding:6px 10px;display:inline-block;font-size:0.95rem;"> 2. Material Requirements </h3> <p><strong>Primary cone material:</strong> Non-pressed long-fiber paper pulp</p> <ul> <li><strong>Damping:</strong> Random fiber geometry reduces high-Q resonances</li> <li><strong>Thermal stability:</strong> Consistent response under sustained load</li> <li><strong>Breakup:</strong> Distributed rather than singular resonance modes</li> </ul> <p><strong>Surround:</strong> Treated cloth or fabric-based suspension</p> <ul> <li>Predictable compliance curve</li> <li>Reduced nonlinear hysteresis vs rubber surrounds</li> </ul> </section> <section style="margin-bottom: 20px;"> <h3 style="background:#000;color:#fff;padding:6px 10px;display:inline-block;font-size:0.95rem;"> 3. Performance Constraints </h3> <ul> <li>No narrowband resonances dominating midrange response</li> <li>Controlled high-frequency breakup above operating band</li> <li>Stable transient decay under high SPL conditions</li> </ul> </section> <div style="margin-top:20px; padding:15px; border:1px solid #ddd; background:#fafafa;"> <strong>Design Doctrine (Non-Engineering Layer):</strong><br><br>

The selection of long-fiber paper is based on distributed damping behavior compared to synthetic polymer cones.
This document defines a constrained design philosophy prioritizing predictable breakup behavior over rigidity or brightness.
