<section style="margin-bottom:20px;">
<h3 style="background:#000;color:#fff;padding:6px 10px;display:inline-block;font-size:0.95rem;">
1. Deployment Scenarios
</h3>

<p>
Dual-node operation enables stereo imaging and expanded coverage in environments requiring spatial sound reproduction.
</p>

<ul>
<li><strong>Performance Use (DJ / Live Sound):</strong> stereo imaging and wide-field coverage for spatial audio content</li>
<li><strong>Studio / Monitoring Use:</strong> stereo reference playback with controlled directivity and consistent imaging</li>
<li><strong>Coverage Expansion:</strong> two units increase effective coverage depending on placement and acoustic environment</li>
</ul>
</section>

<section style="margin-bottom:20px;">
<h3 style="background:#000;color:#fff;padding:6px 10px;display:inline-block;font-size:0.95rem;">
2. Signal and Power Architecture
</h3>

<ul>
<li><strong>Signal Input:</strong> Left/Right discrete channels via balanced XLR connection</li>
<li><strong>Power System:</strong> each cabinet operates independently with its own 36V battery and amplification stage</li>
<li><strong>Grounding:</strong> balanced inputs provide isolation to minimize ground loop issues between units</li>
</ul>
</section>

<section style="margin-bottom:20px;">
<h3 style="background:#000;color:#fff;padding:6px 10px;display:inline-block;font-size:0.95rem;">
3. Deployment Notes
</h3>

<ul>
<li>Low-frequency response depends on room geometry, distance, and phase alignment</li>
<li>Placement symmetry improves stereo imaging stability</li>
<li>Center-field summation effects are environment-dependent and not fixed in gain</li>
</ul>
</section>
